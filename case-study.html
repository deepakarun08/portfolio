Oracle APEX Workflow Redesign

Redesigning complex enterprise approval workflows to improve clarity, reduce errors, and help users complete tasks confidently without relying on training or documentation.

Overview

This project focused on simplifying approval workflows used daily by finance and operations teams, while working within strict enterprise, compliance, and technical constraints.

My Role

UX Design, Research, IA, Interaction Design, Testing

Users

Finance Approvers, Operations Staff, Admin Users

Platform

Oracle APEX (Web)

Duration

8 weeks

Context & Background

The application supported invoice approvals across multiple business units. Although functionally correct, users frequently made mistakes, delayed approvals, and depended heavily on SOP documents and training sessions.

Leadership initially perceived this as a ‚Äútraining issue,‚Äù but usability analysis revealed deeper UX problems.

Understanding the Users

The primary users were experienced professionals, but not technical experts.

  • Time-constrained and multitasking
  • Risk-averse due to financial and compliance impact
  • Low tolerance for ambiguity or errors

A key insight emerged early: users did not want flexibility or customization ‚Äî they wanted clarity, predictability, and confidence.

‚ÄúI just want to know what I should do next, without second-guessing.‚Äù

Problem Definition

Users struggled to complete workflows accurately due to unclear status, inconsistent interactions, and delayed feedback.

  • Workflow status was hard to interpret
  • Errors appeared after submission instead of being prevented
  • Actions behaved differently across modules
  • High cognitive load on every screen

Constraints & Considerations

The solution had to work within significant technical and business constraints.

  • Oracle APEX component limitations
  • Backend workflows could not be redesigned
  • Compliance rules were non-negotiable
  • Minimal disruption to existing users

These constraints shaped a design approach focused on clarity and structure rather than radical UI changes.

Research & Discovery

I combined qualitative and quantitative methods to understand user behavior.

  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Contextual inquiry and observation
  • Heuristic evaluation
  • Error log and support ticket analysis

Analysis showed that most errors occurred due to misinterpreting workflow status, not lack of knowledge.

Design Strategy

I defined four guiding principles that informed all design decisions:

  • Status before action
  • Prevent errors, don‚Äôt explain them
  • One primary action per screen
  • Progressive disclosure for complexity

Solution

The redesigned experience focused on making system intent explicit.

  • Status-first layout at the top of every workflow screen
  • Inline validation to prevent errors early
  • Consistent placement of primary actions
  • Clear confirmation and feedback after actions

Complexity was not removed ‚Äî it was structured and prioritized.

Usability Testing & Validation

Designs were validated using real workflows and real users.

  • 6 moderated usability sessions
  • Task-based testing with live data
  • Observation of errors, hesitation, and confidence

All participants completed tasks successfully without external guidance.

Outcome & Impact

  • 30% faster task completion
  • 40% reduction in user errors
  • Training time reduced from 4 weeks to 2 weeks
  • Fewer support tickets and escalations

Users reported increased confidence and reduced mental effort.

Key Learnings

  • Enterprise users value clarity over flexibility
  • Designing within constraints builds better systems
  • Status visibility is critical in workflow UX

This project reinforced the importance of UX as a communication layer between complex systems and human decision-making.
